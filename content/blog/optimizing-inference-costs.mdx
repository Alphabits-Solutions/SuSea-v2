Token generation is the new infrastructure bottleneck. As large language models move from experimental playgrounds to the backbone of enterprise operations, the marginal cost of each inference call has become the primary constraint on how intelligent your systems can be.

At Susea.ai, we treat inference infrastructure as malleable architecture — not a fixed cost. This analysis covers the precision techniques required to maintain high-fidelity intelligence while aggressively reducing per-query compute cost.

## What Drives Inference Cost?

Before optimising, you need to understand the three levers: **model size**, **hardware utilisation**, and **batching efficiency**. Most teams focus on model selection and ignore the latter two, leaving 60–80% of potential savings on the table.

## Core Methodology

### Dynamic Quantization

Compressing FP32 weights to INT8 or 4-bit precision without sacrificing semantic integrity. Our approach uses per-channel scaling factors to preserve statistical outliers — the values that carry disproportionate semantic weight in attention layers.

**Efficiency gain: 4.2× throughput increase at equivalent quality.**

### Sparse Pruning

Systematically identifying and zeroing non-critical neuronal connections. We target 30% sparsity while maintaining 99.4% of baseline accuracy on task-specific benchmarks. The key is calibrating on your actual production queries, not generic benchmarks.

### KV Cache Optimisation

Implementing PagedAttention to eliminate memory fragmentation. By managing Key-Value caches like virtual memory in an OS, we double the maximum batch size on NVIDIA H100s — which directly translates to lower cost-per-query at scale.

## Infrastructure Efficiency

Deployment strategy is the invisible multiplier. Container orchestration overhead and cold starts can negate model-level gains. We pair a serverless GPU architecture with global load balancing to ensure sub-second queue times even at traffic spikes.

## The Architectural Trade-off: Quality vs. Cost

There is a real trade-off here. Aggressive 4-bit quantization can degrade nuanced reasoning tasks — we do not recommend it for chain-of-thought or tool-use agents. The right strategy is **tiered quantization**: run high-stakes reasoning at FP16, and commodity generation (summaries, formatting, classification) at INT8 or lower.

This tiering, combined with semantic routing to the right model tier, is where most of the practical gains come from in production systems.

## The Strategic Imperative

Efficiency is not merely a cost-saving measure — it is the prerequisite for embedding intelligence everywhere. Lowering the floor of inference costs allows for:

- **Deeper reasoning** — more compute per query without budget overruns
- **More agentic interactions** — multi-step chains become economically viable
- **Ubiquitous AI** — intelligence embedded in every workflow, not just premium tiers

If your inference cost is growing linearly with usage, you have an architectural problem — not a budget problem. The techniques above are production-proven across our client portfolio, delivering an average 60–80% reduction in per-query cost without measurable accuracy degradation on target tasks.
