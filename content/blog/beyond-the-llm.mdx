70% of enterprise AI pilots never reach production. The model is almost never the reason.

The answer lives in the infrastructure surrounding the model — and most teams underestimate how much of it exists.

## The Demo-to-Production Gap

A language model running in a Jupyter notebook is not an enterprise product. Between that notebook and a production system handling 10,000 concurrent users sits a chasm that contains:

- **Observability** — you can't improve what you can't measure
- **Reliability** — fallbacks, retries, and graceful degradation
- **Cost governance** — budget controls and per-request cost tracking
- **Data pipelines** — freshness, quality, and retrieval architecture
- **Security** — authentication, authorisation, and prompt boundaries

The architectural challenge is building all five simultaneously, before you know exactly what the system needs to do at scale.

## What Durable AI Infrastructure Looks Like

### Layer 1: The Data Plane

Everything starts with data. Before a single prompt is written, you need:

1. A clean, versioned, and documented data store
2. A retrieval strategy — vector search, keyword, or hybrid — matched to your use case
3. Freshness guarantees — stale data produces stale responses regardless of model quality

**The trade-off:** Vector search gives semantic flexibility but is expensive to maintain at high update frequency. Keyword search is cheaper and exact, but misses synonyms and context. Most production systems use hybrid retrieval with a re-ranker — optimise retrieval architecture before optimising the model.

### Layer 2: The Orchestration Layer

This is where most teams cut corners. The orchestration layer manages:

- **Context management** — what information reaches the model and in what order
- **Tool use** — how agents interact with external systems and APIs
- **State persistence** — how conversations maintain continuity across sessions

Poor orchestration is why agents hallucinate. The model isn't lying — it's filling gaps left by incomplete context.

### Layer 3: The Evaluation Loop

Production AI systems degrade silently. Without continuous evaluation, you won't know performance has drifted until a customer complains. Build evaluation into your CI/CD pipeline from day one:

1. Define ground-truth test cases representative of your production distribution
2. Run them on every model update or data change
3. Alert when accuracy drops below threshold

## The Architectural Principle

> Build for the failure mode, not the happy path.

Every component needs a defined behaviour when things go wrong:
- LLM API unavailable → fall back to cached response or rule-based handler
- Retrieval returns nothing → respond with a graceful "I don't know"
- Latency exceeds SLA → return partial result with a disclaimer

Teams that answer these questions before launch are the ones with AI in production. The others are still in pilot — and wondering why the demo worked fine.
